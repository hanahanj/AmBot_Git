Prairie du Rocher

This small town was one of the original four or so French Colonial towns founded in the 18th century. First established in 1722 on a tract of land granted by the Royal India Company to Pierre Duque Boisbriant—French commandant at Illinois and builder of Fort Chartres—the town took the name of the rock cliffs against which it was sited—Prairie by the Rock. The town is home to many fine examples of French Colonial architecture including the French-American Creole House, built in a modified poteaux-sur-solle style, which hosts numerous public events throughout the year. The still-active town cemetery dates back to the 18th century, where you can find both old gravestones as well as the old town well. The lively present day town is tightly tucked between the USACE levees and the limestone bluffs, and offers a particularly compact area for exploring how these two features work to form the levee protected zones.
