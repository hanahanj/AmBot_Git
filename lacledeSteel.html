Laclede Steel

To the left, are the sooty, newly resuscitated mills of Laclede Steel (now Alton Steel). Named after the french fur-trader and founder of the city of St Louis, the surname of Pierre Laclede can be found gracing countless institutional, commercial, and civic ventures in the region. From the town and county of Laclede, to an elementary school, to a prominent riverfront landing in downtown St Louis, to the name of the regional gas utility in the St Louis metro area, Laclede has given his name to many institutional offspring. Which is particularly ironic in that he decidedly did not give his name to his four actual children who were birthed by his common-law wife--and who took the name of her former estranged husband for religious reasons.
